1
Acknowledge their offer, don't reject

2
Create urgency without pressure

Wait 48 hours before responding with your counter. During this time, mention to your Ferencváros contact that you have a CM available. The European interest becomes a real fallback, not a bluff.

3
Counter with data

Counter at €460K (not €480K — shows you moved): "Based on comparable transfers (Dønnum at €520K, Iglesias at €550K to Hapoel themselves), we feel €460K is fair. We can structure with performance bonuses — the player believes in himself."

4
The "bridging concession"

If they counter at ~€400K, close the gap by offering a structured deal: €400K base + €50K in achievable bonuses (20 appearances). This lets Hapoel report a "€400K signing" internally while the true deal is €420-450K. Both sides save face.

5
Lock salary independently

Push for €18K/year salary regardless of fee outcome. Salary negotiation is with the buying club — separate it from the transfer fee discussion. Add a €15K signing bonus as a "welcome gift" from the club. This increases your player's total package without affecting reported transfer fee.

  • Don't mention that Marcus has been benched lately — let them discover it if they haven't
  • Don't accept the first counter without at least one round of negotiation — establishes a dangerous precedent
  • Don't name the "European club" unless pressed — vagueness is power
  • Don't split the fee and salary discussion — keep them separate tracks to maximize total package
