MGSR Football Agent Mandate

Review & Recommendations
Date: March 24, 2026
Document: Football Agent Mandate Template (Android & Web)
Purpose: Identify legal issues, drafting errors & missing provisions
This review is not legal advice. All changes should be reviewed by a licensed sports law attorney familiar with FIFA Football Agent Regulations before implementation.

CRITICAL — Fix Before Using

1

FIFA Fee Cap Violation (Clause 6)

Current text
"the player will pay the agent 5% of his salary on monthly basis"
Problem

Under the FIFA Football Agent Regulations (FAR) 2023, agent service fees are capped at:

  • 3% for player representation
  • 6% for club representation
  • 10% for dual representation

The 5% player-side fee violates FIFA FAR Article 15 and could make the fee provision unenforceable or trigger FIFA disciplinary proceedings.

Recommended Fix

Change to 3% or restructure the fee arrangement to comply with FIFA caps.

2

Broken & Ambiguous Sentence (Clause 6, second paragraph)

Current text
"In case the club will pay the commission – the commission will be paid the agent (if there more than 1 agents involved from other agencies, the commission will be paid equally 50-50%."
Problems
  • Missing closing parenthesis
  • Grammar error: "if there more than 1 agents" → "if there is more than one agent"
  • Does not specify what percentage the club pays the agent
  • "50-50% split" — of what total amount? Too vague to enforce
Recommended Fix

Rewrite entirely with specific percentages, proper grammar, and clear terms for multi-agent fee splitting.

3

Missing Confidentiality Clause

Problem

Clause 18 lists "confidentiality" as surviving termination, but there is no confidentiality clause anywhere in the mandate. The mandate references an obligation that was never defined.

Recommended Fix

Add a dedicated confidentiality clause covering player personal data, financial information, negotiation details, and commercially sensitive information.

4

Referenced Annex Does Not Exist (Clause 22)

Current text
"...as confirmed in the attached annex."
Problem

No annex is generated or attached by the PDF template. The mandate references a document that doesn't exist.

Recommended Fix

Either create and attach the annex (independent legal advice waiver) or replace the reference with a self-contained acknowledgment within this clause.

SIGNIFICANT — Fix Before Next Use

5

Penalty Clause May Be Excessive & Vague (Clause 12)

Current text
"the Player shall pay a contractual penalty equal to 20% (in USD or EURO depends how the player get paid) of the player monthly salary. The penalty is cumulative..."
Problems
  • "Cumulative" penalties can stack to extreme amounts — Swiss courts (CO Art. 163) regularly reduce excessive contractual penalties
  • "Revoking or terminating the Mandate at an inopportune time" — what defines "inopportune"? Too vague to enforce
  • "(in USD or EURO depends how the player get paid)" — informal language inappropriate for a legal document
Recommended Fix

Define a clear, fixed penalty amount or cap. Remove vague triggers. Use formal language for currency specification.

6

Post-Term Fee Rights Are Very Broad (Clause 11)

Current text
"the Football Agent remains entitled to the service fee for as long as that employment contract remains in force"
Problem

A player signs a 5-year contract during the mandate term → the agent receives fees for the full 5 years, even if the mandate was only 1 year. This is aggressive and could be challenged as disproportionate. FIFA FAR has specific post-term remuneration rules that may override this.

Recommended Fix

Cap the post-term entitlement to a reasonable period (e.g., the remaining duration of the mandate's original term) or align with FIFA FAR post-term rules.

7

Dual Representation Consent Is Unclear (Clause 9)

Current text
"The Player might need to permit dual representation..."
Problem

"Might need to" is not a legal obligation or consent. FIFA FAR (Articles 12-13) requires specific, written, prior disclosure and consent for dual representation. Without proper consent language, any transaction involving dual representation could be challenged or invalidated.

Recommended Fix

Replace with a clear dual representation disclosure and consent mechanism, or require separate written consent for each transaction (preferred by FIFA).

8

CAS Arbitration Terms May Be Unfair (Clauses 19-20)

Current text
"under an expedited procedure with CAS deadlines reduced by half"
Problems
  • Cutting CAS deadlines in half significantly limits the player's ability to prepare a defense
  • CAS arbitration is expensive — creates access-to-justice issues for lower-earning players
  • FIFA's own internal dispute mechanisms may need to be exhausted first
Recommended Fix

Remove the "deadlines reduced by half" provision. Consider adding FIFA's internal dispute resolution as a first step before CAS.

9

No Provision for Minor Players

Problem

If any represented player is under 18, the mandate needs:

  • Parental/guardian consent and co-signature
  • Compliance with additional FIFA protections for minors
  • Potentially different fee structures
Recommended Fix

Add a clause addressing minor players, or create a separate mandate template for minors.

DRAFTING & GRAMMAR ERRORS

10

Clause 1 — Broken Grammar

Current
"on a valid leagues mentions in the mandate and exclusive basis"
Should be

"on an exclusive basis, limited to the valid leagues mentioned in this Mandate,"

11

Clause 3 — Capitalization Error

Current
"on the Player's behalf, In the valid leagues."
Should be

"on the Player's behalf, in the valid leagues."

12

Clause 6 — Informal Language

Issues
  • "exclude bonuses" → "excluding bonuses"
  • "on monthly basis" → "on a monthly basis"
  • Inconsistent formality — switches between "The Player shall" and "the player will pay the agent"
13

Origin Agent Paragraph — Unclear

Current
"This mandate valid through the player agent."
Problem

Grammatically incorrect and legally ambiguous. Is this a sub-agency, co-representation, or referral?

Should be

Clearly define the relationship (e.g., "This Mandate is entered into through the Player's existing agent...")

14

Gender-Neutral Language

Problem

The mandate uses "his" and "him" throughout. Since MGSR supports women's football, the template should use "their" or "his/her," or define the convention in the preamble.
